We are Apiviva Agrotechno Consult & Supplies Ltd

The company was founded in 2019 by Emmanuel Apiku and Vivian Ababiku Apiku. Four (04) years before, Emmanuel Apiku was working as the staff in the Agricultural Department of a Charitable missionary organization as the Principal Agricultural Extension Officer (PAEO)/Agricultural Livelihood Focal person, he discovered a big problem in meeting the needs of the rural farmers in transforming the rural populace into a sustainable food and income security through the current approaches, these current approaches of training alone would rather impair satisfying the rapidly growing market demands. Emmanuel Apiku later initiated a plan to create their own company which is not only providing Agricultural Advisory Services/training to the rural farmers but also supplies of high quality agricultural inputs, research and consultancy services to farming households/groups and organizations engaged in livelihood and its related activities as well.
Together working with him in this effort is his wife Vivian Ababiku Apiku, Charity organizations and groups that directly or indirectly support rural farmers in fighting food and income security through Agricultural information dissemination/trainings and input supplies.

After they discovered that total charity kills indigenous creativity by breeding an un ending dependency among the rural farmers and total business depletes them off the local resource through profit repatriation that benefits a few members of the value chain, they persuaded each other to incorporate their ideas into a limited liability compony with a dual legal identity of charity and business. Apiviva Agrotechno Consult & Supplies Ltd finally came into a legal existence on the 6th day of May 2019.

Till this day, Apiviva Agrotechno Consult & Supplies Ltd has saved cost of acquiring high quality agricultural inputs for the rural farmers and offered timely quality agricultural advisory services/trainings to its cliental at no cost in the last 2 years directly reaching about 15,000 farming households with its products and services in over 4 districts (Moyo, Adjumani, Obongi and Yumbe) of Northern Uganda, West Nile Region including parts of Southern Sudan (Nimule and Kojo-Keji County).
